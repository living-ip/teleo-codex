# Pli: Crêperie Ambulante Futardio Fundraise

## Summary
Pli Crêperie Ambulante attempted to raise $350,000 on futard.io for a traditional food truck business in Zürich. The project explicitly rejected crypto-native framing, positioning futarchy purely as an alternative capital formation mechanism to traditional fundraising. The raise failed within one day (launched 2026-03-04, closed with Refunding status 2026-03-05), providing a critical data point on futarchy applicability to non-crypto-native physical businesses.

## Market Data
- **Outcome:** Failed (Refunding status)
- **Duration:** 1 day (2026-03-04 to 2026-03-05)
- **Proposer:** Solutions Architect in tech, based in Zürich, not trained chef
- **Target:** $350,000 for food truck, equipment, permits, and one year operations

## Budget Breakdown
- Food truck + fit-out: ~60,000 CHF
- Equipment (billig, fridges, supplies): ~8,000 CHF
- Permits & insurance: ~6,000 CHF/year
- Ingredients & supplies: ~24,000 CHF/year
- Market fees & parking: ~10,000 CHF/year
- Marketing & branding: ~6,000 CHF/year
- Founder living expenses: ~90,000 CHF/year (Zürich cost of living)
- Buffer / contingency: ~15,000 CHF
- **Total:** ~219,000 CHF (~$250K)

## Significance
This represents the first documented attempt to use futarchy-governed fundraising for a traditional consumer food business. The founder explicitly stated: "This isn't a tech startup. There's no AI, no protocol, no flywheel diagram. This is a food truck, a billig (crêpe griddle), and someone who's done the math." The immediate failure suggests futarchy markets filter out non-crypto-native projects rapidly, even on permissionless platforms. This contrasts with Myco Realms' successful $125K mushroom farm raise, which positioned itself as DePIN infrastructure with measurable on-chain metrics.
