# Onchain accountability with founder-led execution is a distinct governance category that keeps DAOs transparency guarantees while restoring the execution speed that token voting destroyed

Stani Kulechov proposes a governance model that is explicitly neither a traditional DAO nor a traditional corporation. The key elements:

**What to keep from DAOs:**
- Rules stay in the code
- Treasury remains visible to everyone
- Token holders retain safeguards on major decisions (protocol changes, treasury strategy)

**What to add from companies:**
- Founders and teams lead execution
- "Someone needs to wake up every morning with the full context in their head and make hard calls"
- Execution decisions don't go through multi-week governance processes

**The differentiator — onchain accountability:**
- All decisions and performance are onchain and transparent
- Token holders can fire the team when objectives aren't met
- "Accountability is verifiable, and that is what separates this from a traditional company"
- "There is no vendor lock-in" — teams are replaceable, not entrenched

This model resolves a specific tension: DAOs achieved transparency and censorship-resistance but sacrificed execution speed and accountability. Traditional companies have execution speed and clear accountability but sacrifice transparency. Stani's proposal keeps the transparency guarantee (onchain, verifiable) while restoring execution speed (founder-led decisions) and adding a new form of accountability (permissionless team replacement).

The claim that "tokens and governance can actually be a solution to public markets that are broken" positions this not as a retreat from decentralization but as an evolution toward better public company governance — where the problems of principal-agent misalignment, opacity, and board capture are solved by onchain transparency and token-holder exit/voice mechanisms.

## Challenges

- This model concentrates execution power in founders, which reintroduces the principal-agent problem that DAOs were designed to solve — the differentiator (onchain accountability + fireable teams) must actually work in practice
- "Token holders can fire the team" has never been credibly demonstrated at scale — governance attacks, voter apathy, and information asymmetry may make this theoretical
- The boundary between "major decisions" (governance) and "everything else" (execution) is where political fights will migrate, not disappear
- Since [[futarchy-governed DAOs converge on traditional corporate governance scaffolding for treasury operations]], Stani's proposed evolution may be convergent with what futarchy-governed systems are discovering independently — but without the mechanism-level protections that [[decision markets make majority theft unprofitable through conditional token arbitrage]]
