# Embedding charitable donations in protocol fee structures creates retention through identity formation, turning traders into evangelists

Launchpet's revenue model routes one-third of every transaction fee to verified animal welfare organizations — applied equally whether trades occur inside the app or on external platforms via the liquidity pool. The design explicitly frames this not as charity theater but as a retention and engagement mechanism: users who benefit real animals by trading develop emotional investment in the platform's success, share their trades socially, and return more frequently than purely financial incentives would produce.

The mechanism exploits identity-based motivation. A user who has donated to animal welfare through their trading activity is no longer a pure speculator — they are a contributor to a cause they can name. This identity stake creates switching costs that financial returns cannot: leaving the platform means leaving the impact, not just the yield. The same logic converts passive users into active promoters, because sharing their trades is now also sharing their virtue signal.

The design is structurally different from corporate ESG or voluntary impact investing. The charitable component is non-optional, automated, and on-chain verifiable — users cannot doubt whether the donation happened. This solves the trust gap that undermines most impact investment narratives (where 92% of investors cite fragmented measurement as a barrier). The protocol's transparency converts what would otherwise be a marketing claim into a cryptographically auditable fact.

If the hypothesis holds, platforms with embedded charitable fee routing should show higher DAU/MAU ratios, more organic referrals, and lower user acquisition costs than equivalent financial-only platforms. This is testable but currently undemonstrated.

## Evidence

- **Primary**: Launchpet pitch via Futardio launch page `BWeT96hGV245sm6Ua4EhLPL8GngcBV2aKS2uvkaEkjBi` (2026-03-05)
- **Fee structure**: 1/3 → token creator, 1/3 → animal welfare orgs, 1/3 → Launchpet DAO. Fee baked into liquidity pool, applies to all trades regardless of venue
- **Framing**: "This isn't charity theater — it's a retention and engagement mechanism that drives sharing, repeat usage, and emotional investment. The impact layer turns every degen into an evangelist."
- **Related context**: ESG trust gap — 92% of investors cite fragmented measurement as a barrier, $19.6B fled US ESG funds in 2024 — suggests on-chain verifiable donation tracking could unlock demand that traditional impact products cannot

## Challenges

- Launchpet has not launched; this is a design thesis without behavioral evidence
- Identity-motivation effects may not transfer to crypto-native audiences who are acculturated to financial maximization and skeptical of virtue signaling
- Charitable routing could backfire if the animal welfare partners lack credibility or the donation amounts are trivially small relative to trading gains
- Users may discount the emotional value of involuntary charity (they didn't choose to donate, the protocol did)
