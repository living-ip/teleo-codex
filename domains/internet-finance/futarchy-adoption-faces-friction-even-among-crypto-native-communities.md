Futarchy's adoption has been limited despite its theoretical elegance and appeal to crypto-native communities. Even organizations like MetaDAO, which are explicitly built around futarchy principles, face challenges in attracting sustained participation.

The friction appears to stem from multiple factors:
- Cognitive overhead of conditional prediction markets
- Lack of familiar mental models from traditional governance
- Bootstrap problem: markets need liquidity to be useful, but liquidity requires existing users
- Uncertainty about whether market prices actually reflect informed judgment vs. speculation

## Relevant Notes:

- **Drift Futarchy incentive allocation (2024-05-30)**: Drift's first futarchy proposal allocated 50,000 DRIFT tokens to incentivize participation, demonstrating that financial incentives were deemed necessary even for experienced futarchy users (Futardio cult members who had already participated in MetaDAO's futarchy experiments).
