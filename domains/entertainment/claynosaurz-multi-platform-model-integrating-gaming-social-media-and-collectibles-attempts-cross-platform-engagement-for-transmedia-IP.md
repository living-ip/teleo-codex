## Core Claim
Claynosaurz's multi-platform model integrating gaming, social media, and collectibles attempts cross-platform engagement for transmedia IP

## Evidence
- Claynosaurz coordinates multiple touchpoints: YouTube episodes, Gameloft mobile game (in co-development), physical/digital drops, and fan co-creation
- Creator Nick Cabana describes the strategy as creating "a unified progression layer across all touchpoints"
- The model aims to allow audiences to engage with the IP across different platforms and formats
- [[community-co-creation-in-entertainment-IP-development-increases-audience-investment-and-reduces-production-risk|Community co-creation]] is integrated into the multi-platform approach

## Challenges
- Single case study (Claynosaurz) limits generalizability
- The achievement system architecture is described but not detailed in available evidence
- The Gameloft mobile game is in co-development, so the integrated system is planned rather than fully operational
- No quantitative data on cross-platform engagement rates or user progression across touchpoints
- Unclear whether the unified progression layer has been implemented or remains aspirational

## Counter-Evidence
- Traditional transmedia franchises often operate in platform silos without integrated progression systems
- Cross-platform achievement systems face technical and business model challenges (platform holder restrictions, revenue sharing)

- Source: Variety article on Claynosaurz creator-led transmedia strategy (2025-10-01)
- The strategy represents an attempt to create cohesive transmedia experiences rather than disconnected platform presences
