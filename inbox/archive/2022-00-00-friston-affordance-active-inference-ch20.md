# Affordance and Active Inference

## Core Argument
Under active inference, affordances are reframed from properties of the external environment (Gibson's original formulation — "the chair affords sitting") to attributes of the agent's action plans and policies. The affordance of sitting is a property of the act of sitting, inseparable from the agent doing it. This aligns affordances with expected free energy: one can evaluate the epistemic and pragmatic affordance of each plausible plan of action in bits (or nats).

## Key Claims for Extraction

### Affordances as Policy Attributes
- Traditional view: affordances are external world properties (the chair affords sitting)
- Active inference view: affordances are properties of plans/policies
- "It is the affordance of sitting, opening or looking that underwrites policy selection"
- Cannot separate the affordance from the acting agent — affordance is inherently enactive

### Expected Free Energy Quantifies Affordance
- Epistemic affordance = information gain from a course of action
- Pragmatic affordance = preference satisfaction (utility)
- Both quantifiable in bits/nats via expected free energy
- "One can effectively cast the value of information in the same currency as pragmatic value"
